# CS330-

**How do I approach designing software?
What new design skills has your work on the project helped you to craft?
What design process did you follow for your project work?
How could tactics from your design approach be applied in future work?**

I approch software design in a similar manner to how I do most things in life and that is piece by piece, 
it can be very overwhelming to have to code somthing like this with all these different features and 
functionality if its not done one at a time. When it comes to the rubric i usually just tackle the first
thing or the requirement that I know will be simple or the least time consuming and can play into the next
requirement.

This project has been especially helpful in figuring out the basics in implimenting lighting, being able 
to add a lighting system that was relatively realistic was no easy feat and took me hours to figure out 
somthing, as a result i can also say that my improvising skills have improved as a result as well.

I followed the piece by piece design as meantioned earlier, there was alot to do and could do in this assignment
so i did what was simple and made the most sense first, and added the other requirements as I went, take the lighting
for exmple that was one of the last things i did before submitting the assignment since i found it to be the most difficult.

I think that designing somthing piece by piece can be applied in pretty much any context especially with coding, there are going 
to be bigger and harder projects out there that i may end up doing professionally or even personally. Coding I find intimidating 
in general and difficult be rewarding once you figure somthing difficult out or have a working piece of code in front of you. 
Breaking the problems down into digestable chunks makes those rewarding experiences much more likely and can help fuel your
drive to complete any work.
